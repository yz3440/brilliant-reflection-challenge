Simple Reflection

Each and every point on the surface of a lit object constantly emits rays of light that spread in all directions. A few of these rays make it into our eyes, which do all the hard work of assembling the rays from many points to generate the image that we see.

Imagine you are standing in front of a mirror, with a light bulb on your side. You will be able to see the light builb from the mirror, right?

Try to reflect the ray to the eye through the mirror.

This is all built from a simple rule

When a ray of light encounters a surface, the angles that the incoming and outgoing light make with the surface are equal.

In other words, the angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.
